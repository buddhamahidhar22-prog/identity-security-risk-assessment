# 🕵️ Identity Security Risk Assessment

## Overview

This project demonstrates a structured identity security investigation conducted following a suspicious account login alert.

The objective was to determine whether historical credential exposure, authentication weaknesses, or identity attack surface risks increased the likelihood of account compromise.

The assessment combined OSINT techniques, breach intelligence analysis, risk assessment, and remediation planning.

---

## Investigation Scope

- Credential Exposure Analysis
- Breach Intelligence Collection
- Identity Attack Surface Assessment
- Authentication Security Review
- Risk Analysis & Threat Modeling
- Security Recommendations

---

## Investigation Workflow

### Phase 1 – Alert Validation

- Review suspicious login notification
- Establish investigation scope
- Identify monitored identities

### Phase 2 – Exposure Intelligence Collection

- Public breach intelligence analysis
- Credential exposure verification
- Historical breach review

### Phase 3 – Authentication Assessment

- MFA review
- Password hygiene analysis
- Recovery pathway assessment

### Phase 4 – Threat & Risk Analysis

- Credential stuffing scenarios
- Account takeover pathways
- Identity compromise risk assessment
- Risk matrix development

### Phase 5 – Remediation Planning

- MFA recommendations
- Password security improvements
- Breach monitoring controls
- Identity governance recommendations

---

## Key Findings

- Historical credential exposure identified across multiple breach sources.
- No evidence of active compromise observed.
- Authentication weaknesses increased potential attack surface.
- Risk posture assessed as Moderate.
- Recommended controls significantly reduced account takeover risk.

---

## Key Learning

A blocked login attempt may be the beginning of an investigation, not the end.

Historical breaches remain operationally relevant years after disclosure and continue to influence identity security risk.
